## What Towing Costs in San Antonio

San Antonio is one of the more affordable large cities in the country for towing. Lower cost of living and strong competition among local operators keep rates below average. A standard tow runs **$70 to $115** for the base fee, plus **$3.00 to $4.50 per mile**. A 5-mile tow typically costs $85 to $138 total. A 10-mile tow comes to $100 to $160.

Even with lower base rates, San Antonio's size means distance can still make your bill climb.

## Typical Towing Costs in San Antonio

- **Hook-up / base fee:** $70 - $115
- **Per-mile rate:** $3.00 - $4.50
- **After-hours tow:** Add $20 - $40
- **Highway breakdown (I-10, I-35, Loop 410):** $95 - $145 base
- **Short local tow (under 5 miles):** $85 - $138 total
- **Medium tow (10-15 miles):** $100 - $183 total

## What Drives the Price Up

**Distance in a spread-out city.** San Antonio sprawls considerably. Towing from the far North Side to a shop near downtown can run 15 or more miles.

**Military traffic.** San Antonio is home to multiple military installations including Lackland AFB, Fort Sam Houston, and Randolph AFB. Heavy vehicle traffic and regular transient drivers in unfamiliar vehicles contribute to more breakdowns and towing calls.

**Highway heat breakdowns.** Texas heat beats vehicles hard. I-10 through San Antonio sees more heat-related breakdowns from summer through fall, and demand ticks up during those months.

**Specialty vehicles.** Lifted trucks and older work vehicles are common in San Antonio. Oversized or heavy vehicles sometimes require heavier-duty equipment and a higher rate.

## How to Avoid Getting Ripped Off

**Texas PUCT regulations apply here.** Non-consent tow rates are regulated. If your vehicle was towed from a private lot or by police without your call, check the applicable rate schedule.

**Shop around by phone.** San Antonio has a lot of towing operators. A quick 2-minute comparison call can save you $20 to $40 on a routine tow.

**Know the difference between a tow and a push.** If your car just needs to be moved 100 feet to a safe spot, you don't need a full tow. Some companies will try to run the full meter on a trivial situation.

**Use your roadside coverage.** Auto insurance towing coverage and AAA are both widely available in San Antonio and cost significantly less than a cash tow.

**Be specific about your vehicle.** If you have a lifted truck or a vehicle with a unique tow point situation, tell the dispatcher upfront. This avoids surprise charges when the driver arrives.

## San Antonio-Specific Notes

TxDOT's HERO program does not have as dense a coverage in San Antonio as in Houston or Dallas, but TxDOT does respond to incidents on I-10 and I-35 corridors. For minor issues on those highways during business hours, a TxDOT response may arrive before a private tow.

San Antonio city impound rates run $120 to $175 for the initial tow plus $20 to $30 per day for storage.
