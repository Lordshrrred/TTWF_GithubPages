> **Quick Answer:** Do not drive with a stuck gas pedal. Pull over immediately using your brakes and parking brake. Turn off the engine and call for roadside help. A stuck accelerator can cause loss of control and serious accidents. Your brakes can overpower the accelerator, but only temporarily.

## What To Do Right Now

1. **Stay calm and grip the steering wheel firmly**. Do not panic or jerk the wheel.

2. **Press your brake pedal hard with both feet if needed**. Your brakes are stronger than the engine and will slow the car down.

3. **Shift into neutral immediately**. This disconnects the engine from the wheels, even if the gas pedal stays stuck.

4. **Pull over to the safest spot possible**. Use your emergency flashers to warn other drivers.

5. **Turn off the engine completely**. Remove the key or push the engine stop button.

6. **Engage your parking brake** before getting out to inspect the pedal area.

7. **Check for obvious obstructions**. Look for floor mats, bottles, or debris under the gas pedal that you can safely remove.

8. **Do not attempt to drive if the pedal remains stuck**. Even if you cleared an obstruction, the throttle mechanism may be damaged.

## Common Causes of Stuck Gas Pedals

Floor mats are the most frequent culprit. Aftermarket mats or loose factory mats can slide forward and trap the accelerator. Worn throttle cables can also stick or bind, especially in older vehicles.

Spilled liquids create sticky residue around the pedal assembly. Ice formation in winter can freeze the throttle linkage. Mechanical failures in the throttle body or accelerator position sensor require professional repair.

## Why You Cannot Drive Home

A stuck accelerator creates an extremely dangerous situation that can worsen without warning. Even if you think you have it under control, the throttle mechanism may fail completely, causing sudden acceleration when you least expect it.

Your brake system will generate heat from constant use while fighting the stuck throttle. This can lead to brake fade or failure, especially on longer drives. The engine will also run at high RPM, risking serious damage from overheating or over-revving.

If you experience other warning signs like [check engine light flashing](/check-engine-light-flashing-can-i-drive-home/), stop driving immediately as multiple systems may be failing.

## What It Might Cost

Towing costs range from $75 to $200 for local service, depending on your location and time of day. The repair itself varies widely based on the cause.

Simple fixes like repositioning floor mats cost nothing. Throttle cable replacement runs $150 to $300. Electronic throttle body problems can cost $400 to $800 to repair.

## Stay Safe

• Never try to fix a stuck pedal while driving
• Keep both hands on the wheel at all times
• Use neutral gear to disconnect power from wheels
• Call for professional help rather than risk driving
• Check your insurance for roadside assistance coverage
