> **Quick Answer:** Towing from interstate to dealership costs $150-500+ depending on distance (typically $3-7 per mile after base fee), time of day, and truck type needed. Most dealerships accept direct payment from insurance roadside coverage. Call your insurance first, then compare with local tow companies.

## What To Do

1. **Pull over safely** on the interstate shoulder, turn on hazards, and exit the vehicle away from traffic if possible.

2. **Call your insurance roadside assistance first**. Most policies cover towing to the nearest qualified repair facility, which includes dealerships. [GEICO roadside assistance towing coverage](/geico-roadside-assistance-towing-coverage-how-much-pays/) and other major insurers often pay $100-150 toward towing costs.

3. **Ask specifically about dealership towing** when speaking with roadside dispatch. Some services require you to go to their preferred shop first, but most allow dealership towing if you request it.

4. **Get distance and cost estimates** before agreeing to service. Interstate to dealership tows average 10-25 miles in most metro areas.

5. **Confirm payment method** with the tow operator. Many accept insurance direct pay, credit cards, or dealership billing arrangements.

6. **Document everything** including your exact interstate location (mile marker), destination dealership address, and any damage to your vehicle before towing.

## What It Might Cost

**Base rates:** $75-150 hookup fee plus $3-7 per mile
**Typical distance:** 10-25 miles from interstate to dealership
**Total range:** $150-325 for standard tows during business hours

**Additional costs:**
- After hours/weekends: Add $50-100
- Flatbed (required for AWD, luxury cars): Add $25-75
- Heavy traffic delays: $50-150 per hour waiting time
- Toll roads: Pass-through costs

**Insurance coverage:** Most roadside policies cover $100-200 of towing costs. [AAA towing coverage](/aaa-towing-coverage-runs-out-what-happens-cost/) typically provides higher limits than standard auto insurance.

**Payment options:**
- Insurance direct billing (most common)
- Credit card on scene
- Dealership billing (some accept, call ahead)
- Cash (always accepted, often gets discount)

## Stay Safe

• Never attempt repairs on interstate shoulder
• Exit vehicle on side away from traffic if safe to do so
• Set up emergency triangles 100+ feet behind your car if you have them
• Stay visible to tow truck driver but away from traffic lanes
• Keep all doors unlocked for tow operator vehicle access
• Take photos of your car's condition before and after towing
• Verify tow truck driver credentials and company name matches who you called

Highway patrol may stop to assist or direct traffic during the tow. Some states charge additional fees for patrol escort services on busy interstates.

If your car needs [emergency towing due to engine problems](/car-making-grinding-noise-when-starting-tow-truck-needed/) or [overheating issues](/car-radiator-leak-steam-coming-out-tow-cost/), request a flatbed to prevent further damage during transport to the dealership.
