## What Towing Costs in Baltimore

Baltimore sits in the expensive Northeast corridor, and towing rates reflect that. A standard tow runs **$90 to $145** for the base fee, plus **$4.00 to $5.25 per mile**. A 5-mile tow typically costs $110 to $171 total. A 10-mile tow runs $130 to $197.

Baltimore's position on the I-95 corridor between DC and Philadelphia means a lot of through-traffic and highway breakdown calls that push demand and rates higher.

## Typical Towing Costs in Baltimore

- **Hook-up / base fee:** $90 - $145
- **Per-mile rate:** $4.00 - $5.25
- **After-hours tow:** Add $30 - $55
- **I-95, I-695, I-83 breakdown:** $120 - $175 base
- **Short local tow (under 5 miles):** $110 - $171 total
- **Medium tow (10-15 miles):** $130 - $224 total

## What Drives the Price Up

**I-95 corridor traffic.** I-95 through Baltimore carries enormous volumes of interstate traffic. Breakdowns on this corridor require elevated safety standards and command premium rates.

**The Fort McHenry Tunnel.** The tunnel on I-95 creates a traffic chokepoint. Vehicles that break down near the tunnel or require a cross-harbor tow face logistical complexity.

**Northeast labor and operating costs.** Baltimore's wages and operating costs are higher than Midwest or Southern cities, which flows into towing rates.

**Urban density.** Baltimore's dense row-house neighborhoods create narrow streets, parking complications, and logistical challenges for tow trucks that add time to every job.

## How to Avoid Getting Ripped Off

**Maryland towing law is specific.** Maryland Transportation Code Title 21 covers vehicle towing. Non-consent tows must follow specific procedures and fee limits.

**Baltimore City has additional ordinances.** The city regulates private property tows. Operators must be licensed and follow posting requirements. If you were towed from a private lot without your consent, verify compliance with city rules.

**Get a written estimate.** Maryland law supports this -- and most reputable Baltimore operators provide it without issue.

**Use your insurance or roadside coverage.** Northeast towing rates make coverage more valuable. Most Maryland auto insurance policies include towing.

**Know the Harbor Tunnel alternative.** The Fort McHenry Tunnel on I-95 carries a toll. If your breakdown is near there or you need a tow through the tunnel area, the routing complexity adds cost.

## Baltimore-Specific Notes

MDTA (Maryland Transportation Authority) manages tolls and has incident response on I-95 and the Beltway. They do not provide free towing, but coordinate with private companies for efficient incident clearance.

Baltimore City impound fees run $150 to $200 for the initial tow plus $30 to $45 per day in storage. The city impound lot is in the Pulaski Highway area.
