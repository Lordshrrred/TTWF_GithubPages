> **Essential Information:** Your primary goal is escaping active traffic lanes at all costs. Signal immediately, reduce speed gradually, and navigate toward the shoulder or nearest exit. Activate hazard lights the instant you detect problems, even before identifying the specific issue. Once positioned safely, remain inside your vehicle with restraints fastened unless you detect flames or smoke. Statistics show that vehicles parked on highway shoulders face frequent collisions from passing traffic.

## Step-by-Step Response Plan

1. **Activate hazard lights without delay.** Switch these on the moment you sense trouble, before troubleshooting or contacting help. Highway-speed traffic requires maximum advance warning.

2. **Navigate toward the right shoulder.** When possible, take the next available exit to leave the highway completely. Otherwise, target the rightmost shoulder, positioning yourself as distant from active lanes as feasible. Damaging a wheel rim beats stopping in traffic flow.

3. **Position yourself maximally rightward.** Move beyond the solid white boundary line. When a guardrail exists, park 6-12 inches away from it. Each additional foot of separation from traffic significantly improves safety.

4. **Remain inside with restraints secured.** Though this seems wrong, it's the safest approach. Your vehicle provides better visibility than a person standing nearby. Seatbelts offer protection during potential secondary collisions. Only exit if you detect smoke or flames.

5. **When exiting becomes necessary, create maximum distance.** Use the passenger door (traffic-side avoidance), scale any guardrail present, and position yourself uphill or behind protective barriers. Never position yourself between your car and moving traffic.

6. **Contact emergency services first when in immediate danger.** For situations involving lane blockage, traffic interference, or limited sight distance locations, dial 911 before towing services. Provide precise location details: specific highway, travel direction, closest mile marker or exit identifier.

7. **Subsequently contact towing or roadside services.** Share identical location information with them. Many major highways feature state-operated assistance patrols offering free help when available.

8. **Display clear distress signals.** Attach white fabric or light-colored bags to your driver-side door handle or antenna as a universal help request visible to law enforcement.

## Critical Safety Information

Highway shoulder areas present substantial dangers. Research indicates that disabled vehicles on shoulders experience frequent strikes from passing traffic, particularly during nighttime or adverse weather conditions. Your safest location remains inside your vehicle, properly restrained, positioned at maximum distance from traffic flow while awaiting professional assistance.

Disable interior lighting after dark to improve hazard light visibility for approaching drivers. Maintain hazard light operation continuously since most vehicles can power these systems for 1-2 hours without creating starting difficulties, making battery conservation unnecessary.
