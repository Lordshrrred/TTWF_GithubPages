## What Towing Costs in Denver

In Denver, a standard local tow typically runs **$85 to $135** for the base hook-up fee, plus **$3.50 to $5 per mile** after that. For a 5-mile tow to a nearby shop, you're looking at roughly $100 to $160 total. That's the realistic range -- not the inflated quote you'll get if you call the first number on Google at midnight.

Denver sits at a higher cost of living than the national average, and that carries over into towing rates. The city also has a lot of mountain-adjacent highway traffic, which means companies here price in the occasional vehicle recovery from a ditch or ravine.

## Typical Towing Costs in Denver

- **Hook-up / base fee:** $85 - $135
- **Per-mile rate:** $3.50 - $5.00
- **After-hours or late-night tow:** Add $25 - $50 on top
- **Highway or I-25/I-70 breakdown:** $120 - $175 base (elevated risk for the driver)
- **Short local tow (5 miles or less):** $100 - $160 total
- **Medium tow (10-15 miles):** $140 - $220 total

## What Drives the Price Up

**Distance** is the biggest factor. Every mile past the first adds to your bill, and Denver's metro sprawl means a tow from Aurora or Lakewood to a shop in the city can rack up 10+ miles quickly.

**Time of day** matters. After 9pm and before 7am, most Denver tow companies charge a premium. Same goes for weekends and holidays.

**Vehicle type** affects cost too. Lifted trucks, all-wheel drive vehicles, and low-clearance sports cars often require a flatbed, which costs more than a standard wheel-lift tow. Flatbed rates in Denver start around $110 and go up.

**Location of breakdown** is a factor. Stuck on I-70 in a construction zone or on I-25 near downtown? That tow is going to cost more than a simple call from a parking lot.

## How to Avoid Getting Ripped Off

**Get a quote upfront before they hook up your vehicle.** In Colorado, tow companies are required to give you a written estimate before towing. If a driver refuses or gets cagey about pricing, call a different company.

**Ask specifically about the per-mile rate.** The base fee looks reasonable until you realize the mileage charge doubles the bill. Know both numbers.

**Know your destination.** The closer the shop to your breakdown, the less you pay. Keep a list of shops near common routes you drive.

**Check your insurance and membership.** Many auto insurance policies cover towing. AAA membership includes free tows up to a certain distance. Call your insurer before paying out of pocket.

**Avoid dispatch services that resell calls.** Some "towing" numbers online are actually middlemen who take a cut and send whoever is available. You often end up with higher prices and slower response times.

## Denver-Specific Notes

The Denver metro area includes a lot of highway driving on I-70, I-25, I-225, and C-470. Breakdowns on these corridors are common, especially in winter. CDOT contracts with specific towing companies for highway incidents, and those rates are regulated -- but if you call a private company yourself, you're on your own for pricing.

Parking enforcement tows in Denver (called by the city or a lot) are a different situation. Those fees are set and you pay at the impound lot, typically $140 to $200 plus storage fees after the first day.
