> **Quick Answer:** Highway to nearest exit towing typically costs $75-$150 for distances under 5 miles. Base hookup fees run $50-$75, plus $2-$5 per mile. Add 25-50% for night, weekend, or emergency rates. Police-required tows from highways often cost more due to faster response requirements.

## What To Do

1. **Call 911 if unsafe** - If your car is blocking traffic or you're in immediate danger, call emergency services first. They'll dispatch police and arrange towing.

2. **Move to shoulder if possible** - Get your car as far right as safely possible. Turn on hazard lights immediately.

3. **Get distance quotes upfront** - Tell the tow company you need transport to "the nearest safe exit" and ask for the exact mileage and total cost before they arrive.

4. **Choose your exit wisely** - The nearest exit isn't always cheapest if it's only 0.5 miles but has no services. An exit 2-3 miles away with gas stations and repair shops often makes more sense.

5. **Check if police dispatch is required** - On many highways, only police can call tow trucks for safety reasons. This limits your choice of company but ensures faster response.

6. **Document everything** - Take photos of your location markers or mile posts. Tow companies may charge based on where they think you are, not where you actually broke down.

7. **Ask about drop-off fees** - Some companies charge extra if the exit doesn't have a safe place to leave your vehicle overnight.

## What It Might Cost

**Base towing fees:**
- Hookup: $50-$75
- Per mile: $2-$5 
- Short highway pulls (1-3 miles): $75-$125
- Longer pulls to exit (4-7 miles): $100-$175

**Additional charges:**
- Night/weekend: Add 25-50%
- Emergency highway response: Add $25-$50
- Flatbed (recommended): Add $10-$25
- Waiting time: $50-$75 per hour

**Police-dispatched tows** often cost 15-25% more but arrive faster. If you're dealing with [other emergency situations like engine problems](/car-overheating-red-light-dashboard-what-to-do/), the faster response is worth the extra cost.

Most exits within 5 miles will keep your total under $150 during normal hours. For longer distances, [towing from interstate to specific destinations](/towing-cost-from-interstate-to-dealership/) can get expensive quickly.

Urban highways typically have closer exits but higher base rates. Rural highways may require longer tows to reach services, but often at lower per-mile rates.

## Stay Safe

- Never exit your vehicle on the traffic side
- Stay behind barriers or guardrails while waiting
- Keep phone charged for multiple calls if needed
- Have cash ready - some highway tow operators prefer cash
- Don't agree to repairs at the roadside - focus on getting to safety first
- If you have [ongoing mechanical issues like grinding noises](/car-making-grinding-noise-when-starting-tow-truck-needed/), mention this to the tow operator for proper equipment selection

Highway breakdowns create time pressure, but getting an exact quote prevents surprise bills. Most reputable companies will honor their phone quotes even if the actual distance varies slightly.
