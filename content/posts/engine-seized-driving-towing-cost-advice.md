When your engine suddenly locks up while you're driving, panic can set in quickly. This mechanical nightmare requires swift action to protect both your safety and your wallet. Understanding the costs involved and knowing exactly what steps to take can make this stressful situation much more manageable.

> **Emergency Response:** Stop driving the moment your engine seizes. Expect towing expenses between $75-200 locally, potentially $500+ for longer hauls. Get to safety, shut off the motor, contact professional towing services. Never try restarting a seized engine as this creates additional costly damage.

## Immediate Actions Required

When engine seizure strikes, follow these critical steps:

1. **Get to safety fast** as soon as you notice the engine binding or hear metal-on-metal sounds
2. **Shut down the motor completely** and resist any urge to turn the key again
3. **Activate emergency flashers** and navigate toward the roadside when feasible
4. **Contact professional towing services** or your roadside coverage provider
5. **Remain inside your car** when positioned near heavy traffic until rescue arrives
6. **Take photos of the situation** to support any insurance claims later
7. **Arrange transport to a qualified repair shop** for professional assessment

## Expected Financial Impact

**Professional Towing Rates:**
- Short haul service (within 10 miles): $75-150
- Moderate distance (10-25 miles): $150-250
- Extended transport (beyond 25 miles): $200-500+
- Flatbed transport upgrade: Additional $25-50
- Holiday/night service: Extra 25-50% premium

**Potential Repair Expenses:**
- Complete engine swap: $3,000-8,000+
- Professional engine rebuild: $2,500-5,000
- Initial inspection fee: $100-150

## Safety Protocols

**During rescue wait time:**
- Maintain flashing hazard signals
- Stay inside when positioned near active traffic lanes
- Exit away from traffic if safely parked
- Position warning triangles 100+ feet behind your vehicle when available
- Contact emergency services if traffic poses immediate threats
