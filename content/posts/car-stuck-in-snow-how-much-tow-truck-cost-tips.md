Winter weather can trap your vehicle in snow faster than you expect. When this happens, knowing your options and understanding towing costs helps you make smart decisions about getting back on the road.

**At a Glance:** Snow recovery services typically run $75-200 for straightforward extractions, $150-400 for winch-assisted pulls from ditches, and $200-500+ for complex recoveries. Pricing depends on location distance, equipment needed, and timing. Always attempt self-extraction safely first, verify insurance coverage, and compare quotes from local operators.

## Immediate Action Steps

1. **Remain inside your vehicle** when stranded on roadways. Activate emergency flashers and request assistance from the safety of your heated car.

2. **Attempt basic self-rescue methods**. Remove snow buildup around wheels, beneath the vehicle, and from the exhaust outlet. Place floor mats, sand, or kitty litter under tires for grip.

3. **Use gentle rocking motion**. Alternate between forward and reverse while applying minimal throttle. Avoid aggressive tire spinning which can worsen your situation.

4. **Verify roadside service benefits** through your auto insurance or AAA membership before hiring private towing. Winter assistance is often included in these plans.

5. **Request pricing estimates** from several local recovery services when paying personally. Costs can differ substantially between operators.

6. **Describe your predicament accurately** during phone calls. A vehicle "trapped in shopping center snow" requires different equipment than one "rolled into a roadside ditch."

7. **Prepare payment method**. Recovery operators typically expect immediate cash or credit card settlement upon completion.

## Expected Recovery Expenses

**Simple extraction services:** $75-150
- Direct pullout from parking areas or private driveways  
- Standard tow vehicle attachment with straight-line recovery

**Winch-assisted operations:** $150-300
- Vehicles buried in substantial snow or shallow ditches
- Requires cable winching equipment and extended work time  

**Complex recovery situations:** $200-500+
- Cars in deep ditches or overturned positions
- Demands multiple vehicles or specialized recovery gear

**Mileage charges:** $3-7 per mile beyond standard service radius
**Off-hours premiums:** 25-50% additional fees for nights/weekends/holidays  
**Interstate incidents:** Generally more expensive due to traffic management requirements

## Winter Safety Protocol

- Maintain emergency supplies including blankets, bottled water, non-perishable food, and portable phone battery
- Never leave your engine idling unattended in snowy conditions (carbon monoxide hazard)
- Maintain visibility: keep emergency flashers active, raise hood when circumstances permit
- Avoid walking along busy highways to signal for assistance
- If trapped overnight, operate engine for 10-minute intervals each hour for warmth, keeping a window slightly open
- Ensure exhaust pipe remains snow-free to prevent dangerous gas accumulation
