## What Towing Costs in Minneapolis

Minneapolis has seasonal towing economics unlike almost any other major American city. In summer, rates are moderate. In winter, demand spikes and so do prices. A standard tow runs **$85 to $140** for the base fee, plus **$3.75 to $5.00 per mile**. A 5-mile tow typically costs $104 to $165 total. A 10-mile tow runs $123 to $190.

Winter tows often carry premium charges due to weather, ice, and the extra work involved in freeing a vehicle from snow.

## Typical Towing Costs in Minneapolis

- **Hook-up / base fee:** $85 - $140
- **Per-mile rate:** $3.75 - $5.00
- **After-hours tow:** Add $25 - $50
- **Highway breakdown (I-94, I-35W, I-394):** $110 - $165 base
- **Winter weather / snow tow premium:** Add $30 - $60
- **Short local tow (under 5 miles):** $104 - $165 total
- **Medium tow (10-15 miles):** $123 - $215 total

## What Drives the Price Up

**Minnesota winters.** Minneapolis winters are severe, with temperatures regularly dropping below zero and significant snowfall from November through March. Cold weather means more dead batteries, more accidents, more vehicles stuck in snow, and more demand for tow service.

**Snow emergency parking rules.** Minneapolis declares Snow Emergencies when significant snowfall occurs. During a snow emergency, vehicles parked on designated routes get towed. Tens of thousands of vehicles are towed each winter under this program. The fees are set by the city.

**Vehicle recovery from snow.** Getting a vehicle unstuck from a ditch or deep snow requires more work than a simple mechanical breakdown tow. Recovery tows carry a higher rate.

**Limited after-hours availability.** During severe cold snaps, tow demand can exceed capacity. Wait times increase and rates may go up.

## How to Avoid Getting Ripped Off

**Know the Snow Emergency parking rules.** The city posts Snow Emergency routes online and sends alerts. Signing up for city notifications prevents a surprise tow to an impound lot.

**Minnesota has consumer protection for towing.** MN Statute 168B governs vehicle towing and storage. Operators must provide itemized invoices and follow fee regulations for non-consent tows.

**Get a quote upfront for any private tow.** Summer or winter, always confirm base fee and mileage rate before agreeing.

**AAA is particularly valuable in Minneapolis.** Minnesota's winters make roadside assistance coverage one of the best investments a driver can make. Winter battery replacements, tows, and lockouts are all covered.

**Cold-weather preparation reduces tow calls.** Keep battery terminals clean, maintain your antifreeze, and keep winter traction items in your car. This doesn't prevent every breakdown, but it reduces the odds.

## Minneapolis-Specific Notes

Minneapolis Snow Emergencies are declared when 3 or more inches of snow are expected. Snow Emergency tow fees are $155 for the tow plus $30 to $50 per day storage at city impound. The impound lot is at 51 Colfax Avenue North. You can check the Minneapolis 311 app to find your vehicle.

MnDOT operates a highway service patrol program on Twin Cities metro freeways during peak hours. These trucks can provide minor assistance but do not replace a private tow for shop transport.
