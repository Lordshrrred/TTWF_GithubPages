> **Bottom Line:** Strange sounds from your steering system typically indicate issues with power steering components, CV joints, or wheel bearings. Metal grinding sounds require immediate attention. Other noises can usually wait for short trips but shouldn't be ignored.

## Immediate Steps to Take

1. **Determine the sound type:**
   - Whining/squealing: Usually power steering fluid issues or belt problems
   - Clicking/popping: Likely CV joint wear (especially noticeable in tight turns)
   - Metal grinding: Wheel bearing damage or brake component wear
   - Moaning/groaning: Power steering pump malfunction

2. **Inspect power steering fluid** (with engine cool and off):
   - Find the reservoir near your engine bay
   - Check fluid level against minimum and maximum marks
   - Replace if fluid appears dark or has a burned odor

3. **Perform stationary test:**
   - With engine running, turn steering wheel fully left and right while parked
   - Determine if sounds occur only during movement or also when stopped

4. **Road test observations:**
   - Note if noise happens exclusively during turns
   - Identify which turning direction creates more noise
   - Monitor if sound intensity changes with vehicle speed

5. **Cease driving when:**
   - Steering response becomes sluggish or difficult
   - Grinding sounds intensify
   - Steering wheel shakes excessively

## Repair Cost Estimates

- Power steering fluid refill: $5-15
- Complete power steering system flush: $80-150
- Single CV joint repair: $300-600
- Power steering pump replacement: $200-500
- Individual wheel bearing service: $150-400
- Power steering belt installation: $50-120

## Safety Considerations

Don't dismiss steering system sounds as minor annoyances. When power steering fails, vehicle control becomes significantly more difficult, particularly during low-speed maneuvers and parking situations. Any grinding noises or sudden steering difficulty means you should stop driving immediately and contact emergency roadside service.
