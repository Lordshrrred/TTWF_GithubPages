> **Quick Answer:** After-hours towing in Phoenix during winter typically costs $150-400 for local distances. Winter rates include 25-50% surcharges for nights, weekends, and holidays. Distance, vehicle size, and specific location affect final pricing.

## What To Do

1. **Call multiple tow companies** for quotes before choosing. Prices vary significantly between operators.

2. **Ask for the total cost upfront** including base fee, mileage, after-hours charges, and any winter weather surcharges.

3. **Verify they can reach your location.** Some mountain areas around Phoenix become inaccessible during winter storms.

4. **Get arrival time estimates.** Winter weather and after-hours staffing can delay response by 1-3 hours.

5. **Confirm payment methods accepted.** Many after-hours services require cash or credit cards, not checks.

6. **Stay with your vehicle if safe** or move to a visible, warm location nearby while waiting.

## What It Might Cost

**Base after-hours towing (5-15 miles):**
- Standard cars: $150-250
- SUVs/trucks: $175-300
- Heavy vehicles: $250-400

**Additional charges:**
- After-hours fee: $25-75
- Winter weather surcharge: $30-50
- Mileage over base: $3-7 per mile
- Difficult access: $50-150

**Most expensive scenarios:** Remote locations during snowstorms on holidays can reach $500-600.

## Stay Safe

• Keep your phone charged and bring a portable charger
• Dress warmly and have blankets in your car during winter months
• Keep emergency water and snacks in your vehicle
• Turn on hazard lights and use flares or reflectors if you have them
• Never leave your car on a busy road, even in winter weather
• If stranded in snow, stay with your vehicle rather than walking for help
• Keep your exhaust pipe clear of snow to prevent carbon monoxide poisoning
• Run your engine periodically for heat but crack a window for ventilation

Phoenix winter storms are rare but can create dangerous conditions quickly. Desert temperatures drop fast after sunset, making hypothermia a real risk even in Arizona.
