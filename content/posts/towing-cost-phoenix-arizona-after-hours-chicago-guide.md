> **Quick Answer:** After hours towing in Phoenix runs $150-400 depending on distance and truck type. Chicago costs $175-450 for the same service. Both cities charge premiums for nights, weekends, and holidays. Local tow companies often beat AAA response times but cost more upfront.

## What To Do

1. **Call local tow companies first** - They respond faster than AAA during peak breakdown hours
2. **Ask for flat rate pricing** - Avoid per-mile charges that add up quickly on long hauls
3. **Confirm the total cost** before they hook up your car - No surprises at drop-off
4. **Get cash ready** - Most after hours services want payment on the spot
5. **Have your location ready** - Cross streets, landmarks, mile markers help dispatchers find you faster

## Phoenix After Hours Towing Costs

**Standard rates (6 PM - 6 AM, weekends, holidays):**
- Local tow (under 10 miles): $150-225
- Medium distance (10-25 miles): $200-300  
- Long haul (25+ miles): $300-400
- Flatbed service: Add $25-50

**Chicago After Hours Towing Costs:**
- Local tow (under 10 miles): $175-250
- Medium distance (10-25 miles): $225-350
- Long haul (25+ miles): $350-450
- Downtown pickup: Add $50-75 surcharge

## What It Might Cost

Phoenix averages 15% cheaper than Chicago for equivalent services. Chicago's higher insurance costs and stricter licensing drive prices up. Both cities charge 40-60% more after hours compared to daytime rates.

AAA members pay nothing upfront but wait 45-90 minutes during busy periods. Private companies arrive in 20-45 minutes but cost $150-450 out of pocket.

## Stay Safe

• Pull completely off the road with hazard lights on
• Stay in your vehicle if traffic is heavy
• Keep doors locked until the tow truck arrives
• Verify the company name matches who you called
• Don't sign blank work orders or credit card slips
• Take photos of your car before it gets loaded
