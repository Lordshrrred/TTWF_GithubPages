> **Emergency Response:** When your vehicle suddenly stops running while you're driving, remain composed. Your braking system still has one power-assisted stop left, and steering remains possible though both will require more force quickly. Use your turn signal, guide your car toward the roadside, and brake to safety. Activate emergency flashers immediately. Never attempt to restart the engine while moving. After reaching a safe stop, then investigate the problem. Typical culprits include: fuel starvation, alternator breakdown, fuel pump malfunction, or timing belt failure.

## Immediate Action Steps

1. **Stay composed.** Your vehicle retains enough forward motion for steering and stopping. The car won't seize up completely.
2. **Activate turn signals and head for the shoulder.** On highways, move toward the right lane instantly and exit the roadway completely.
3. **Press brakes with extra force.** Power-assisted braking still functions but feels rigid, requiring additional pressure beyond normal operation.
4. **Use both hands on the steering wheel.** Power steering has failed. The wheel responds but demands significantly more physical effort.
5. **Position your vehicle completely off the travel lanes.** Get wheels onto the shoulder, angling the front end slightly away from passing traffic when possible.
6. **Activate emergency flashers.** Switch these on immediately after stopping.
7. **Attempt one restart.** Use your key or push-button starter. If successful, monitor all dashboard indicators, particularly temperature and fuel readings.
8. **When restart fails**, contact roadside help or towing services. Avoid repeated starting attempts without understanding the root cause.

### Probable Failure Points

| Root Cause | Warning Signs That Preceded Failure |
|---|---|
| Empty fuel tank | Low fuel indicator active, engine sputtering initially |
| Alternator breakdown | Battery warning illuminated, electrical systems flickering |
| Fuel pump malfunction | Engine hesitation and rough running before shutdown |
| Broken timing belt | Complete sudden stop with no advance warning |
| Engine overheating | Rising temperature readings on gauge |
| Faulty ignition switch | Abrupt power loss without dashboard warnings |

## Repair Cost Expectations

| Component | Price Range |
|---|---|
| Alternator service | $400 – $800 |

| Timing belt service | $500 – $1,000 |
| Ignition switch repair | $150 – $350 |

When timing belt failure occurs, avoid any restart attempts. Interference-type engines risk severe internal damage from broken timing belts. Professional assessment is required before attempting ignition.

## Safety Priorities

- **Remain inside your vehicle on busy highways until traffic clears.** Keep your seatbelt fastened on high-speed roads. Contact emergency services if your position creates danger.
- **Deploy warning devices** like flares or reflective markers 200 feet behind your disabled vehicle if available.
- **For smoke or burning odors**, evacuate all occupants immediately and distance yourselves from the vehicle. Never open the hood of a smoking car.
- **During nighttime incidents**, turn on interior lighting after setting hazards to increase visibility for oncoming drivers.

Vehicles that die during operation rarely have simple roadside solutions. Arrange for professional towing, explain the circumstances to your repair facility, and allow them to perform proper diagnostic procedures.
