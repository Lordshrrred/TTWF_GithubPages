> **Quick Answer:** Police-ordered motorcycle towing typically costs $150-400 for the tow plus $20-50 daily storage. You can often request a specific tow company if your bike is rideable and not blocking traffic. Police may require immediate removal if your motorcycle is a road hazard.

## What To Do

1. **Ask the officer if you can arrange your own tow** if your motorcycle isn't blocking traffic or leaking fluids. Many departments allow this.

2. **Get the tow company name and lot address** from the officer before they leave. Write it down or take a photo of their business card.

3. **Ask about storage fees and pickup hours** immediately. Some lots charge from the moment your bike arrives, others start the next day.

4. **Take photos of your motorcycle** before it's loaded if possible. Document any existing damage beyond the accident.

5. **Call your insurance company** as soon as safe. They may cover towing costs and can sometimes redirect to their preferred facility.

6. **Retrieve your bike within 24-48 hours** if possible. Storage fees add up fast and some states allow tow yards to file liens after 30 days.

7. **Bring cash or call ahead about payment** methods. Many tow lots don't accept cards, especially after hours.

## What It Might Cost

- **Towing fee**: $150-400 depending on distance and bike size
- **Storage**: $20-50 per day, starting immediately or next business day
- **After-hours pickup**: Additional $50-100 fee
- **Administrative fees**: $25-75 for paperwork and lot processing

Large touring bikes and trikes cost more than sport bikes or cruisers due to weight and special equipment needed.

## Stay Safe

- Never argue with police about towing decisions at an accident scene
- Don't ride a damaged motorcycle even if it starts
- Keep tow receipts for insurance claims
- Inspect your bike thoroughly before accepting it back from storage
- Ask about any additional damage that occurred during towing
