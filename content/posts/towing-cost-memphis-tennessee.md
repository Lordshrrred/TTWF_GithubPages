## What Towing Costs in Memphis

Memphis is one of the more affordable major cities for towing in the South. Lower operating costs and strong local competition keep rates below the national average. A standard tow runs **$70 to $115** for the base fee, plus **$3.00 to $4.25 per mile**. A 5-mile tow typically costs $85 to $136 total. A 10-mile tow runs $100 to $158.

## Typical Towing Costs in Memphis

- **Hook-up / base fee:** $70 - $115
- **Per-mile rate:** $3.00 - $4.25
- **After-hours tow:** Add $20 - $40
- **I-40, I-240, I-55 breakdown:** $95 - $143 base
- **Short local tow (under 5 miles):** $85 - $136 total
- **Medium tow (10-15 miles):** $100 - $179 total

## What Drives the Price Up

**Memphis bridge complexity.** The Memphis area has multiple Mississippi River crossings, and getting from one side to the other adds distance and routing complications.

**Weather.** Memphis gets winter weather -- ice storms and occasional snow -- that creates surges in towing demand. During these events, wait times and rates both increase.

**Major highway crossroads.** I-40, I-55, and I-240 converge in Memphis. The city is a major freight and logistics hub, meaning heavy commercial traffic and more highway incidents.

**After-hours demand.** Memphis has an active nightlife, tourism (Beale Street, the music scene), and a 24-hour commercial shipping economy. After-hours towing is common and consistently carries a premium.

## How to Avoid Getting Ripped Off

**Tennessee towing laws apply.** Non-consent tows from private property are regulated. If your vehicle was towed without your permission, there are fee limits in place.

**Know the Memphis impound system.** Memphis has had historical issues with impound procedures and fees. Know where the city lots are and what documentation you need to retrieve a vehicle.

**Get a quote upfront.** Memphis towing is affordable compared to other cities, but "affordable" doesn't mean unlimited. Get the base fee and mileage rate confirmed before agreeing.

**Use your roadside coverage.** In a city where towing rates are already moderate, using your included coverage is easy savings.

**Avoid driving through construction zones without caution.** Memphis has ongoing infrastructure projects. Construction zone breakdowns are more complicated and sometimes more expensive.

## Memphis-Specific Notes

TDOT does not operate a free highway service patrol in Memphis. TDOT incident management coordinates emergency response on I-40, I-55, and I-240, but private tow companies handle the actual towing.

Memphis impound fees run $110 to $165 for the initial tow plus $20 to $30 per day in storage. The MPD manages the impound process, and having all your paperwork ready speeds retrieval.
