## What Towing Costs in Tucson

Tucson is notably more affordable than Phoenix for towing. A smaller market, lower labor costs, and strong competition among local operators keep rates reasonable. A standard tow runs **$75 to $120** for the base fee, plus **$3.00 to $4.25 per mile**. A 5-mile tow typically costs $90 to $141 total. A 10-mile tow runs $105 to $163.

The biggest risk in Tucson is the summer heat. When it's 105 degrees, you need a tow fast, and urgency is exactly what some operators count on.

## Typical Towing Costs in Tucson

- **Hook-up / base fee:** $75 - $120
- **Per-mile rate:** $3.00 - $4.25
- **After-hours tow:** Add $20 - $40
- **I-10 or I-19 breakdown:** $100 - $148 base
- **Short local tow (under 5 miles):** $90 - $141 total
- **Medium tow (10-15 miles):** $105 - $184 total

## What Drives the Price Up

**Summer heat.** Tucson's summer is brutal, with temperatures from June through August regularly hitting 100 to 110 degrees. Heat-related breakdowns spike during these months, and demand pushes wait times and rates up.

**Monsoon season.** Arizona's monsoon season (July through September) brings sudden intense storms that can flood streets, trap vehicles, and create more demand for towing.

**University of Arizona traffic.** UA brings significant student traffic to Tucson. Move-in weekends, football games, and graduation periods create notable spikes in traffic and breakdowns.

**Mountain and desert terrain.** Tucson is surrounded by mountains. Vehicles that attempt mountain roads unprepared -- inadequate cooling system, worn tires -- often need recovery from off-road or difficult terrain situations.

## How to Avoid Getting Ripped Off

**Arizona towing regulations under ARS 28-4901 apply.** Non-consent tows have regulated fee schedules. Know these if your vehicle was towed without your call.

**Get a quote before they hook up.** In extreme heat, the temptation is to just agree to anything to get the process moving. Resist. A 2-minute price check can save $30 to $50.

**Use ADOT's Freeway Service Patrol.** ADOT operates a free Freeway Service Patrol on I-10 near Tucson. If you break down on I-10 during certain hours, a ADOT truck may reach you first and push you to safety at no charge.

**Car prep for desert driving.** Check your cooling system before summer. A thermostat, water pump, or radiator that's marginal elsewhere will fail in Tucson summer heat.

**Know where the shops are.** Near I-10 exits is a good default. Tucson has auto shops near most interstate exits, which keeps mileage costs lower.

## Tucson-Specific Notes

ADOT's Freeway Service Patrol covers portions of I-10 in the Tucson area. This service provides minor assistance and will push your vehicle to a safe location at no charge, though it does not transport to a repair shop.

Pima County impound fees run $105 to $155 for the initial tow plus $15 to $25 per day in storage.
