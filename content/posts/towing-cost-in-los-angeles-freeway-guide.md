> **Quick Answer:** Los Angeles freeway towing costs $150-350 base hookup fee plus $3-8 per mile. CHP-authorized operators charge premium rates on freeways. Expect 25-50% surcharges for nights, weekends, and rush hour. Distance to drop-off location significantly impacts your total cost.

## What To Do

1. **Move your car to the shoulder safely** if possible. Turn on hazard lights and exit from the side away from traffic.

2. **Call 911 if you're in immediate danger** or blocking traffic lanes. CHP will coordinate towing on state freeways.

3. **Contact your insurance roadside assistance first** before calling a tow truck directly. Many policies cover freeway breakdowns.

4. **Get the exact mile marker location** to tell the dispatcher. This speeds up response time and helps with accurate quotes.

5. **Ask for total cost upfront** including hookup fee, mileage, and any surcharges. Request flatbed service for luxury vehicles or AWD cars.

6. **Verify the tow truck operator is CHP-authorized** for freeway work. Unlicensed operators cannot legally tow from California freeways.

7. **Confirm your destination** before they hook up your car. Changing destinations mid-tow adds significant costs.

## What It Might Cost

**Base hookup fees** range from $150-250 during normal hours, jumping to $200-350 for nights and weekends. The 405, 101, and 110 freeways command higher rates due to traffic difficulty.

**Distance charges** add $3-8 per mile from pickup to drop-off. A 20-mile tow from downtown LA to Pasadena costs $210-410 total.

**Surcharges** apply for rush hour (25% extra), nights after 8 PM (35% extra), and weekends (50% extra). Flatbed service adds $50-100 to base rates.

**Storage fees** at tow yards start at $25-40 per day if you can't immediately retrieve your vehicle.

Similar to [towing costs on toll roads](/towing-cost-toll-road-breakdown-emergency-lane/), freeway breakdowns involve additional complications that drive up prices compared to surface street tows.

## Stay Safe

• Stay inside your vehicle if you cannot exit safely away from traffic
• Keep your seatbelt on until help arrives if remaining in the car
• Exit passenger side if broken down in the left lanes
• Stand behind barriers or far from your vehicle once you exit safely
• Never attempt repairs on active freeway lanes
• Keep emergency kit with water, especially during summer months when temperatures exceed 90°F

**Peak traffic periods** (6-10 AM, 3-7 PM) create the most dangerous conditions and highest towing costs. If your breakdown isn't urgent, consider waiting until traffic subsides.

**CHP authorization** matters significantly for freeway tows. Unauthorized operators face steep fines, which they pass to customers through inflated rates. [Construction zone breakdowns](/towing-cost-construction-zone-police-required/) follow similar protocols requiring authorized operators only.

Weather rarely impacts LA towing costs unlike [winter storm surcharges in Chicago](/towing-cost-chicago-winter-storm-surcharge/), but summer heat can increase response times as tow trucks prioritize air-conditioned vehicles first.

The 405 between LAX and the Valley consistently shows the highest towing rates due to traffic density and limited shoulder space. The 110 through downtown LA runs close second for premium pricing.
