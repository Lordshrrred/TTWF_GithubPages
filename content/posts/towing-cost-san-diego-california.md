## What Towing Costs in San Diego

San Diego's towing market reflects California's higher operating costs but is somewhat more affordable than Los Angeles. A standard tow runs **$95 to $155** for the base fee, plus **$4.25 to $5.75 per mile**. A 5-mile tow typically costs $116 to $184 total. A 10-mile tow runs $138 to $213.

San Diego's geography -- spread across valleys, hills, and coastal corridors -- means towing logistics are more complex than a flat grid city.

## Typical Towing Costs in San Diego

- **Hook-up / base fee:** $95 - $155
- **Per-mile rate:** $4.25 - $5.75
- **After-hours tow:** Add $35 - $60
- **I-5, I-8, I-805 breakdown:** $125 - $180 base
- **Short local tow (under 5 miles):** $116 - $184 total
- **Medium tow (10-15 miles):** $138 - $241 total

## What Drives the Price Up

**California costs.** As with all California cities, minimum wage, commercial insurance, and fleet fuel costs are high. San Diego operators carry those costs into their rates.

**Geography.** San Diego's terrain includes beaches, valleys, mesas, and canyons. Getting between neighborhoods can require significant routing. A tow from El Cajon to Pacific Beach might look short on a map but take much longer to drive.

**Military traffic.** San Diego has a massive military presence. Base proximity creates a constant flow of military personnel vehicles -- many driven by young, first-time drivers -- that results in a higher-than-average breakdown and towing call rate.

**Cross-border complications.** San Diego is a border city. Vehicles that break down after crossing from Tijuana or heading toward the border face unique complications and sometimes higher rates.

## How to Avoid Getting Ripped Off

**California's Berman Law protects you.** Non-consent tows are regulated. If your vehicle was towed without your permission from a private lot or by law enforcement, the fees are capped.

**CHP rotation list for highway tows.** If you break down on I-5, I-8, or I-805, the CHP may dispatch a rotation list company. Those rates are regulated. You can also request your own tow company instead.

**Ask about the California fee schedule.** Under AB 2210, non-consent tower fees are regulated statewide. Even private tows -- if initiated without your consent -- must comply.

**Use manufacturer or insurance roadside.** In a California city, this is always the smart play. The difference between paying out of pocket and using your coverage can be $150 to $200 on a single call.

**Know your beaches.** Parking at San Diego beach lots has specific towing enforcement, and beach area operators are well-practiced at quick impoundment. Know the posted rules at any beach parking area.

## San Diego-Specific Notes

California Highway Patrol operates on all San Diego-area interstates and has a rotation tow program. For breakdowns on the freeway where police respond, a rotation company arrives at regulated rates. The company is assigned -- you do not choose it -- but you can always request a different company at additional cost.

San Diego city impound fees run $175 to $225 for the tow plus $35 to $50 per day in storage.
