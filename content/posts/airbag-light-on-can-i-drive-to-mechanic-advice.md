> **Quick Answer:** You can usually drive to the mechanic with an airbag light on, but your airbags won't work in a crash. Drive carefully, avoid highways, take surface streets, and get it checked within 24 hours. Never ignore this warning.

When your airbag light comes on, your airbag system is disabled. The airbags will not deploy in a crash, leaving you and your passengers without this critical safety protection.

## What To Do

1. **Check if the light is solid or flashing**. A solid light means the system detected a problem and shut down. A flashing light indicates the system is running a diagnostic check.

2. **Drive directly to the nearest mechanic**. Do not make stops or delay the trip. Your safety margin is gone until this gets fixed.

3. **Use surface streets instead of highways**. Avoid high-speed driving where crashes are more severe. Take the safest, most direct route.

4. **Make sure everyone wears seatbelts properly**. With airbags disabled, seatbelts are your only crash protection.

5. **Drive more defensively than usual**. Leave extra following distance, check blind spots twice, and avoid aggressive drivers.

6. **Turn off the radio and eliminate distractions**. You need full attention since your safety backup system is down.

The airbag light works like other dashboard warning systems. Just as you need to know [what to do when your check engine light is flashing](/check-engine-light-flashing-can-i-drive-home/), understanding airbag warning signals helps you make smart driving decisions.

## Common Causes

Airbag lights trigger for several reasons. Low battery voltage confuses the airbag computer. Faulty seat sensors think nobody is sitting there. Clock spring failures in the steering wheel disconnect the driver airbag. Crash sensors get dirty or damaged. Wiring connections corrode over time.

Sometimes the light comes on after jump-starting a dead battery or after minor fender-benders that didn't deploy the airbags but triggered the sensors.

## What It Might Cost

Diagnosis typically runs $100 to $150. Simple fixes like replacing a fuse cost under $50. Clock spring replacement ranges from $200 to $500. Airbag module replacement can cost $800 to $1,500. Sensor replacement usually falls between $150 and $400.

Get a diagnosis first. Many airbag light problems come from loose connections or low voltage, not expensive component failures.

## When Not To Drive

Stop driving immediately if multiple warning lights come on together. If the airbag light flashes rapidly while driving, pull over safely and shut off the engine. If you smell burning plastic or see smoke, get out and call for help.

Similar to [transmission fluid leaking situations](/transmission-fluid-leaking-can-i-drive-to-mechanic/), some car problems require immediate attention while others allow careful driving to get professional help.

## Stay Safe

• Keep both hands on the wheel at all times
• Avoid tailgating and aggressive lane changes  
• Choose routes with lower speed limits
• Consider having someone follow you if possible
• Do not attempt to reset the light by disconnecting the battery
