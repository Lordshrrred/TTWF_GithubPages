> **Quick Answer:** A grinding noise when starting usually means starter motor failure, flywheel damage, or severe engine problems. Stop trying to start immediately. If the engine won't turn over or makes metal-on-metal grinding sounds, you need a tow truck to prevent costly damage.

## What To Do

1. **Stop turning the key immediately** when you hear grinding. Continued attempts can destroy your flywheel or starter.

2. **Try starting once more** after waiting 30 seconds. If grinding persists or gets worse, don't try again.

3. **Check your oil level** with the dipstick. Bone-dry oil can cause grinding from seized engine parts.

4. **Listen to the grinding location**. Under the hood usually means starter problems. Deep inside the engine points to internal damage.

5. **Call for a tow truck** if the grinding continues or the engine won't start. Drive-on-demand services won't help with major mechanical failures.

6. **Document the symptoms** for your mechanic: when the grinding started, how long it lasted, and any other unusual sounds.

## What Causes The Grinding

**Starter motor problems** create grinding when the starter gear can't engage the flywheel properly. This happens when starter solenoids fail or the gear teeth wear down.

**Flywheel damage** occurs when a failing starter repeatedly grinds against the flywheel teeth. Once flywheel teeth break off, you'll hear grinding every time you start.

**Engine seizure** from lack of oil creates grinding as metal parts scrape together. This requires immediate towing and often engine replacement.

**Timing chain issues** can cause grinding if the chain jumps or breaks, allowing pistons to hit valves.

## What It Might Cost

Tow truck service runs $75-200 for local trips. Starter replacement costs $300-800. Flywheel repair adds $500-1,500. Engine seizure can mean $3,000-8,000 in repairs or replacement.

## Stay Safe

• Never keep trying to start when you hear grinding sounds
• Don't attempt jump-starting a grinding engine
• Have the car towed to avoid driving with potential engine damage
• Get a second opinion for expensive diagnoses over $1,000
