## What Towing Costs in Boston

Boston is one of the most expensive cities in the country for towing. High labor costs, complex street layouts, and aggressive parking enforcement all push rates up. A standard tow runs **$100 to $165** for the base fee, plus **$4.50 to $6.00 per mile**. A 5-mile tow typically costs $123 to $195. A 10-mile tow can reach $145 to $225.

If your car gets towed in Boston, whether by the city or a private company, you're going to feel it.

## Typical Towing Costs in Boston

- **Hook-up / base fee:** $100 - $165
- **Per-mile rate:** $4.50 - $6.00
- **After-hours tow:** Add $40 - $65
- **I-93, I-90 (Mass Pike), Storrow Drive breakdown:** $135 - $195 base
- **Short local tow (under 5 miles):** $123 - $195 total
- **Medium tow (10-15 miles):** $145 - $255 total
- **Boston street parking enforcement tow:** $130 - $200 (set by city)

## What Drives the Price Up

**Massachusetts labor costs.** Massachusetts has a high minimum wage, high cost of living, and a strong labor market. Tow operators pay more for workers here.

**Boston street complexity.** Boston's streets are notoriously winding and narrow -- they were laid out on old cow paths. Getting a tow truck in and out of many neighborhoods is genuinely difficult and adds time to every call.

**Winter parking bans.** Boston declares parking bans during snowstorms. Vehicles left on the street during a declared ban get towed immediately. The city tows aggressively and quickly.

**Parking enforcement.** Boston has some of the most active parking enforcement in the country. Expired meters, resident permit violations, and street cleaning violations all result in tows.

## How to Avoid Getting Ripped Off

**Massachusetts General Law Chapter 266 Section 120D covers towing.** Non-consent tow fees are regulated. If you were overcharged on an impound tow, you have legal recourse.

**Know the snow emergency rules.** Sign up for Boston city notifications. When a snow emergency is declared, cars on snow emergency routes get towed within minutes. Know which streets in your neighborhood are snow routes.

**For city impound tows:** The fee is set. You pay, you get your car. If you believe the tow was improper, file a complaint through the Boston Transportation Department.

**For private tows:** Get the base fee and mileage rate confirmed before they touch your car. Boston has enough operators that you can compare quickly.

**Resident permit parking:** Know your zone and its hours. Parking in a resident zone without a permit is one of the most common tow triggers in Boston.

## Boston-Specific Notes

Boston city impound (the "tow lot") is in South Boston on D Street. It operates limited hours, so call the BTD tow hotline at (617) 635-3900 to confirm your vehicle is there before making the trip. Hours are typically 7am to 10pm, seven days a week.

Impound fees run $130 to $200 for the tow plus $25 to $40 per day in storage. Cash and credit are accepted but the process is bureaucratic -- bring your registration, license, and proof of insurance.
