> **Quick Answer:** Towing from construction zones with police involvement costs $200-500+ due to traffic control requirements, safety protocols, and potential equipment needs. Police may be required for safety reasons, adding $100-200 in fees. Expect higher rates during peak hours and on major highways.

## What To Do

1. **Turn on hazard lights immediately** and try to move your vehicle to the safest possible location within the work zone barriers.

2. **Call 911 first** if you're blocking traffic or in immediate danger. Construction zones require police coordination for safety.

3. **Stay in your vehicle if safe** or exit away from traffic if you must get out. Construction zones have limited safe areas.

4. **Contact your insurance roadside assistance** before calling a private tow company. Many policies cover construction zone incidents.

5. **Document the scene** with photos showing your vehicle's position relative to construction barriers and signage.

6. **Expect extended wait times** as tow trucks need police escort or traffic control to safely reach you.

7. **Ask the tow operator about construction zone surcharges** upfront, as these fees vary significantly by location and complexity.

## What It Might Cost

**Base towing:** $150-300 for standard hookup and first few miles

**Police traffic control:** $100-200 additional fee in most jurisdictions

**Construction zone surcharge:** $50-150 for safety equipment and extended hookup time

**After-hours premium:** Add 25-50% if breakdown occurs at night

**Specialty equipment:** Extra $100-200 if barriers need to be moved or heavy-duty equipment is required

Total costs typically range $300-600, with major highway construction zones potentially reaching $700+ during peak traffic hours.

Your [towing cost from interstate to dealership](/towing-cost-from-interstate-to-dealership/) may be covered partially by insurance, but construction zone fees often fall outside standard coverage. Check with your carrier about specific work zone provisions.

## Stay Safe

• Never attempt to change a tire or make repairs in an active construction zone
• Keep hazards on and stay visible to approaching traffic and construction workers  
• Exit your vehicle only if absolutely necessary and move away from traffic flow
• Follow instructions from police officers and construction flaggers exactly
• Be prepared for lane closures that may delay the tow truck's arrival

Construction zones create unique challenges for towing operations. The combination of reduced lane space, active work crews, and heavy traffic means [tow truck operators need police coordination](/tow-truck-cost-accident-scene-police-report-required/) to safely access your vehicle. This coordination drives up costs but prevents serious accidents.

Emergency responders prioritize safety over speed in these situations. A breakdown that might take 30 minutes to resolve on a regular highway can easily stretch to 90 minutes in a construction zone while crews coordinate lane closures and traffic control.
