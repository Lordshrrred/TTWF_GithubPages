## What Towing Costs in Columbus

Columbus has a large, competitive towing market driven in part by Ohio State University and the heavy traffic that a major research university and its athletic programs generate. A standard tow runs **$75 to $120** for the base fee, plus **$3.00 to $4.25 per mile**. A 5-mile tow typically costs $90 to $141 total. A 10-mile tow comes to $105 to $163.

Columbus rates are comfortably below the national average for a metro its size.

## Typical Towing Costs in Columbus

- **Hook-up / base fee:** $75 - $120
- **Per-mile rate:** $3.00 - $4.25
- **After-hours tow:** Add $20 - $40
- **I-270, I-71, I-70 breakdown:** $100 - $148 base
- **Short local tow (under 5 miles):** $90 - $141 total
- **Medium tow (10-15 miles):** $105 - $184 total

## What Drives the Price Up

**OSU game days.** On Ohio State home football Saturdays, the entire Columbus metro is affected. Traffic surges, breakdown calls surge, and tow companies charge premium rates during the before and after rush. If you can avoid driving near campus on a game day, do it.

**I-270 Outerbelt volume.** Columbus's I-270 beltway is one of the busiest freight corridors in Ohio. Towing from I-270 or I-71 or I-70 carries a highway premium.

**Ohio winters.** Columbus gets meaningful winter weather. Ice and snow create more accidents and more breakdowns from October through March.

**Suburban growth.** Columbus has expanded significantly. Towing from Dublin, Westerville, or Grove City to a downtown shop can be 15+ miles.

## How to Avoid Getting Ripped Off

**Ohio towing is governed by ORC 4513.** Non-consent tows are regulated and operators must provide itemized invoices. Overcharging on non-consent tows is illegal.

**Columbus has city towing ordinances too.** Private property tows must meet city requirements including proper signage. If you were towed from an apartment lot or commercial parking area without your consent, review Columbus City Code for your rights.

**Get a quote.** Columbus has enough operators that you have options. A quick call comparison saves money.

**OSU students and staff:** Know that your parking permit does not protect you from private lot enforcement in the surrounding neighborhoods. This is a common and expensive surprise.

**Check your coverage.** Ohio auto insurance commonly includes towing riders. Full-coverage policies almost always include it.

## Columbus-Specific Notes

ODOT operates the OHGO traffic management system and has incident response vehicles on the I-270 Outerbelt and other major Columbus-area corridors. These trucks can assist with minor issues but do not tow to repair shops.

Columbus impound fees run $110 to $165 for the initial tow plus $20 to $30 per day in storage. The city impound lot is at 420 W. Whittier Street.
