> **Essential Answer:** Winter driving demands specific emergency supplies: portable jump starter or jumper cables, snow brush with ice scraper, emergency blanket, compact shovel, traction aids like sand or kitty litter, reliable flashlight, and car phone charger. Most motorists realize they need these items only after getting stranded. Assemble your winter emergency kit before harsh weather arrives.

## Getting Started

Prepare your emergency supplies during autumn months. Avoid the rush and potential shortages that occur when winter storms are already forecast.

### Getting Unstuck and Moving

1. **Folding snow shovel**, essential for clearing snow around trapped wheels
2. **Sand, rock salt, or commercial traction mats**, provides grip under slipping tires
3. **Accurate tire pressure gauge**, dropping temperatures reduce tire pressure and compromise traction
4. **Snow chains or cable chains**, mandatory in certain mountain regions; verify local requirements

### Power and Communication

5. **Battery jump pack**, independent starting power when stranded; maintain monthly charging schedule
6. **Heavy-duty jumper cables**, minimum 20-foot length allows flexible vehicle positioning
7. **Vehicle phone charger**, maintains communication capability; store permanently in glove compartment

### Body Heat and Protection

8. **Space blanket (mylar type)**, lightweight, compact heat retention; carry one per occupant
9. **Spare winter gloves and warm hat**, essential for any outdoor work in cold conditions
10. **Chemical hand warmers**, provide 8+ hours of warmth; stock multiple packages

### Safety and Visibility

11. **Ice scraper with snow brush**, store inside passenger compartment to prevent freeze-up
12. **LED emergency flares or reflective triangles**, safer than traditional flares in windy snow conditions
13. **Reflective safety vest**, increases visibility when working near traffic

### Additional Equipment

14. **Compact first aid supplies**
15. **Non-perishable energy snacks**, sustenance during extended waits for assistance
16. **Local area paper map**, backup navigation when phones fail or lose signal
17. **Multi-purpose duct tape**, temporary repairs for hoses, seals, or other components

## Budget Planning

Expect to invest $80–$150 for a comprehensive winter emergency kit. The portable jump starter represents the largest expense at $60–$120. Remaining items cost relatively little. Consider this investment against typical towing fees ($75–$150) or overnight lodging costs when your vehicle becomes immobilized.

## Emergency Procedures

If stranded during severe weather, remain with your vehicle. Cars are easily spotted by rescue teams; people wandering in blizzards are not. Operate your engine in 10-minute intervals each hour for heating, avoiding continuous operation that wastes fuel and risks carbon monoxide poisoning.

Keep a window slightly open when running the engine during heavy snowfall. Accumulated snow can obstruct your exhaust pipe, forcing dangerous CO gases into the cabin. Always verify your tailpipe is clear before starting in deep snow conditions.

Inform someone about your planned route and estimated arrival time before traveling in severe winter weather. This enables rescuers to locate you quickly if you fail to reach your destination.
