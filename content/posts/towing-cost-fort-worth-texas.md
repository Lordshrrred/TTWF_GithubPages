## What Towing Costs in Fort Worth

Fort Worth shares the DFW metro area with Dallas but has its own towing market and slightly lower rates, reflecting the city's somewhat lower operating costs and more spread-out layout. A standard tow runs **$75 to $120** for the base fee, plus **$3.00 to $4.50 per mile**. A 5-mile tow typically costs $90 to $143 total. A 10-mile tow runs $105 to $165.

## Typical Towing Costs in Fort Worth

- **Hook-up / base fee:** $75 - $120
- **Per-mile rate:** $3.00 - $4.50
- **After-hours tow:** Add $20 - $40
- **I-30, I-35W, I-820 breakdown:** $100 - $148 base
- **Short local tow (under 5 miles):** $90 - $143 total
- **Medium tow (10-15 miles):** $105 - $188 total

## What Drives the Price Up

**Cross-metro tows.** Fort Worth is in the western half of DFW. A tow from Fort Worth to a shop in Dallas can easily be 25 to 35 miles -- significant mileage at $3 to $4.50 per mile.

**Industrial and commercial traffic.** Fort Worth has significant industrial areas, including DFW Airport proximity, Alliance Airport, and major distribution centers. Heavy commercial vehicle traffic creates more road wear and more highway incidents.

**Texas heat.** Like all Texas cities, Fort Worth summers create more heat-related breakdowns. June through September sees elevated towing demand.

**Highway construction.** North Texas has ongoing highway expansion and construction on I-35W, I-30, and SH-183. Construction zones complicate towing and sometimes add to response times.

## How to Avoid Getting Ripped Off

**Texas PUCT regulations apply.** Non-consent tow rates are regulated. Know the rate caps for your vehicle class if a tow was initiated without your call.

**Fort Worth vs. Tarrant County rules.** If your tow happens outside city limits in Tarrant County, different ordinances may apply than within the Fort Worth city limits.

**Compare providers.** Fort Worth has enough towing operators that phone comparison is fast and effective.

**Insurance first.** Texas auto insurance and roadside plans are widely held in Fort Worth. Use your coverage.

**Know DFW Airport rules.** If you break down on airport property, airport authority has specific contracted towing services. You do not get to choose a random provider in that situation.

## Fort Worth-Specific Notes

TxDOT's HERO program covers portions of the DFW metro highway system including sections of I-35W and I-30 near Fort Worth. For highway breakdowns during peak hours, a HERO truck may respond before a private company.

Fort Worth city impound fees run $115 to $165 plus $20 to $30 per day in storage. The Fort Worth Police Department manages impound operations.
