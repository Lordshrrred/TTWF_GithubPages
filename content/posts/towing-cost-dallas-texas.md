## What Towing Costs in Dallas

Dallas has a large, competitive towing market with generally lower rates than coastal cities. A standard local tow runs **$75 to $125** for the base fee, plus **$3.00 to $4.50 per mile**. A 5-mile tow typically costs $90 to $148 total. For 10 miles, expect $105 to $170.

Like Houston, the main cost trap in Dallas is distance. The metro area is enormous, and what feels like a local tow can easily turn into a 15 or 20-mile haul.

## Typical Towing Costs in Dallas

- **Hook-up / base fee:** $75 - $125
- **Per-mile rate:** $3.00 - $4.50
- **After-hours tow:** Add $20 - $45
- **Highway breakdown (I-35E, I-635, I-30):** $100 - $155 base
- **Short local tow (under 5 miles):** $90 - $148 total
- **Medium tow (10-15 miles):** $105 - $193 total

## What Drives the Price Up

**Metro sprawl.** Dallas-Fort Worth is one of the largest metro areas in the US. If your shop is across town or in a suburb like Plano, Frisco, or Arlington, the mileage adds up fast.

**Highway demand.** I-35E, I-635 (LBJ Freeway), I-30, and the Dallas North Tollway all see heavy traffic and regular breakdowns. Highway tows cost more than neighborhood tows.

**Ice and winter weather.** Dallas gets ice storms more than people expect. When ice hits, everyone is stuck at once, and tow companies are working overtime with higher rates.

**Vehicle type.** Lifted trucks are extremely common in Dallas, and some of them are too heavy for standard wheel-lift equipment. A flatbed is needed, which bumps the price.

## How to Avoid Getting Ripped Off

**Texas Public Utility Commission regulates non-consent tow rates.** If your vehicle was towed without your permission from a private lot or by police, there are fee caps in place. Know those limits.

**Ask for itemized pricing.** In Texas, tow companies must provide a written invoice. Make sure you see the base fee and mileage rate as separate line items.

**Call before they start.** A 2-minute phone call to compare rates can save you $25 to $50. Most Dallas tow companies will quote over the phone.

**Think about your destination.** If you break down on I-35E, calling a shop near the exit is cheaper than towing to a dealer on the other side of the city.

**Verify your insurance covers it.** Many Texas drivers have towing coverage and never use it because they forget. Check your card or call your agent before paying out of pocket.

## Dallas-Specific Notes

TxDOT operates a Highway Emergency Response Operator (HERO) program on Dallas metro highways. HERO vehicles patrol major corridors during peak hours and can push your car to a safe shoulder or nearby exit at no charge. They won't tow to a shop, but they can get you out of a dangerous spot.

Storage fees at Dallas-area impound lots run $25 to $40 per day after the first day.
