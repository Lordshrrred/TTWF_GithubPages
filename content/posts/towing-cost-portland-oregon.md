## What Towing Costs in Portland

Portland sits in a similar price range to Seattle, reflecting the broader Pacific Northwest cost environment. A standard tow runs **$90 to $145** for the base fee, plus **$4.00 to $5.25 per mile**. A 5-mile tow typically costs $110 to $171 total. A 10-mile tow comes to $130 to $198.

Portland's multiple bridges over the Willamette River add a layer of complexity to towing logistics that doesn't exist in most cities.

## Typical Towing Costs in Portland

- **Hook-up / base fee:** $90 - $145
- **Per-mile rate:** $4.00 - $5.25
- **After-hours tow:** Add $30 - $55
- **I-5 or I-84 breakdown:** $115 - $170 base
- **Short local tow (under 5 miles):** $110 - $171 total
- **Medium tow (10-15 miles):** $130 - $224 total

## What Drives the Price Up

**Pacific Northwest wages.** Oregon has a high minimum wage and the Portland metro area adds additional labor market pressure. Tow companies pay their drivers more here, and that's reflected in rates.

**Bridge and waterway navigation.** Getting a tow truck from the east side to the west side of Portland during rush hour, or navigating around the Willamette bridges, adds real time and complexity to service calls.

**Terrain.** Portland's west hills and surrounding terrain create challenging towing situations for vehicles that break down on inclines or off standard road surfaces.

**Rain.** Portland's perpetual wet season from October through June creates more road incidents, more demand for towing, and more complicated vehicle recovery situations.

## How to Avoid Getting Ripped Off

**Oregon has towing consumer protections.** ORS 646A.375 governs non-consent towing in Oregon. Fees for tows initiated without your consent are regulated, and overcharging is illegal and reportable.

**Portland has specific private property tow rules.** The city requires signage, fee disclosures, and limits on what private lot operators can charge. If you were towed from an apartment or commercial lot, review Portland City Code Title 16 for your rights.

**Get a quote.** Before any work begins, ask for base fee, per-mile rate, and any additional charges. Reputable Portland operators will give you this without issue.

**Your insurance likely covers it.** Oregon drivers with full coverage almost always have towing included. Check before paying out of pocket.

**Plan for bridge delays.** If you break down on a bridge or in a situation where accessing you requires navigating bridge traffic, expect longer wait times and factor that into your plans.

## Portland-Specific Notes

ODOT manages Oregon highways and has incident response vehicles that patrol I-5 and I-84 in the Portland metro. For minor issues during peak hours, ODOT response may be available. For shop tows, a private company is still needed.

Portland impound lot fees for city-initiated tows run $130 to $190 for the initial tow plus $25 to $40 per day in storage. The city has multiple tow-in facilities.
