> **Fast Facts:** When your transmission starts slipping, you'll experience engine revving without corresponding acceleration, hesitation when changing gears, jerky shifting patterns, acrid burning odors, and grinding sounds during gear changes. Pull over immediately and seek professional help to avoid complete transmission destruction.

## Immediate Action Steps

1. **Find safe parking** - Move to a secure location as soon as slipping symptoms appear. Additional driving causes irreversible internal damage.

2. **Inspect fluid levels** - Examine transmission fluid for quantity and quality. Burnt fluid appears dark chocolate or black with a harsh smell.

3. **Document the problems** - Record exactly when slipping happens: while speeding up, at particular velocities, or in certain gear ranges.

4. **Perform basic test** - If driving becomes necessary, shift from Park into Drive in a safe spot. Hesitation beyond 2 seconds signals transmission slippage.

5. **Arrange professional towing** - Avoid driving to repair facilities. Slipping transmissions deteriorate rapidly under driving stress.

6. **Schedule expert evaluation** - Contact transmission specialists rather than general auto shops. Quick action prevents catastrophic failure.

## Expected Repair Expenses

- **Professional diagnosis**: $100-200
- **Fluid replacement/filter service**: $150-300
- **Small component repairs**: $300-800
- **Complete rebuild**: $2,500-4,000
- **New transmission**: $3,000-5,000

Quick intervention prevents expensive disasters. Continuing to drive with slipping symptoms typically converts manageable $500 fixes into $4,000 overhauls.

## Safety Considerations

Don't dismiss these critical warning signals. Total transmission breakdown creates dangerous roadside emergencies. Maintain charged communication devices and track your position when problems begin. Secure backup transportation options before complete transmission failure occurs.
